The chart shows key trends in the gaming industry:

  • Global video game sales peaked between 2008-2010, driven by PS3, Wii, and Xbox 360, alongside the rise of online multiplayer gaming.
  • Sony and Nintendo consistently dominated sales, with PS2 and Wii playing significant roles in the mid-2000s.
  • Sega was a key player in the 1980s-1990s, but declined after the late 1990s with the end of the Dreamcast.
  • After 2010, console sales declined, reflecting market maturity and the rise of mobile, PC gaming, and digital/streaming platforms.
  • Microsoft gained prominence with the Xbox platforms, marking its entry into the console market.

Key takeaway: Time marketing around console cycles is crucial, as sales peaks in the mid-2000s and late 2000s align with the release of major consoles like the PS2, PS3, and Wii. Marketing campaigns should coincide with new hardware launches for maximum impact.
